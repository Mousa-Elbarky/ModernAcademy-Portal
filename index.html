Department Mission and Objectives

The mission of the computer engineering and information technology program: the preparation of a distinguished elite able to compete scientifically and professionally at the local, regional and international levels by providing students with knowledge, concepts and technological skills developed in a framework of cultural, social and Ethical values. The graduate of this program should possess the qualities and satisfies the requirements needed by both public and private sectors.

The objectives of the Computer Engineering and Information Technology Program: The program aims at providing future engineers of computer engineering and information technology with appropriate theoretical knowledge and technical skills to respond to professional market demands. It also provides them with a solid foundation to develop their skills needed for a wide range of professional engineering careers. It provides a balance of theoretical, design and practical subjects which allows them to exploit their individual talents.

The Library

The department is proud to host the specialized Computer Engineering Library, which serves as the primary academic resource for our students and faculty. The library is curated to support our curriculum and to foster research and innovation in various fields of technology.

Key Collection Areas:

Our library houses an extensive collection of hundreds of books, references, and journals covering all essential areas of computer engineering, including:

  • Programming & Languages: Featuring books on C++, Java, Python, PHP, and Android development.
  • Computer Networks & Security: With key resources on data communications, network security, and Cisco technologies.
  • Artificial Intelligence: A growing collection covering AI, machine learning, and neural networks.
  • Computer Architecture & Embedded Systems: Including foundational texts on microprocessors and computer organization.
  • Databases & Operating Systems: Comprehensive guides on database management, SQL, and OS concepts.
  • Software Engineering & Design: Covering software project management, system analysis, and design principles.

The library provides a quiet and conducive environment for study and is an essential partner in the academic success of our students, from their first-year courses to their final graduation projects.
